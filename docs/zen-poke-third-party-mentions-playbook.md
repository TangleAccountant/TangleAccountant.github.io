# Zen Poke Third-Party Mentions Playbook

Last updated: April 2, 2026

This document is the practical outreach playbook for getting Zen Poke mentioned on third-party sites that can help with:

- search legitimacy
- AI-answer visibility
- brand/entity validation
- app discovery
- eventual download growth

This is not a generic "build backlinks" plan.

The goal is:

- get Zen Poke named on relevant third-party domains
- get reviewed, listed, or discussed by sites people and AI systems may trust
- increase the odds that ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google can verify the game outside of `funnymanfinance.com`

## 1. Core Strategy

Priority order:

1. get editorial or reviewer coverage on sites that already talk about mobile, cozy, indie, or puzzle games
2. get Zen Poke into respected game databases and external entity pages
3. keep supporting that with community posts and comments that feel real, not spammy

Do not:

- mass-email dozens of unrelated sites
- pay for random "backlink packages"
- ask for dofollow links
- send the same generic pitch everywhere
- frame Zen Poke as a medical or therapeutic product

## 2. The One-Sentence Pitch

Use this as the base description everywhere:

`Zen Poke is a calming and relaxing mobile game for phones and tablets where players poke through soft felt-style layers to reveal cozy scenes one touch at a time. It is built as a gentle, stress-relief-friendly experience for short breaks.`

Short backup version:

`Zen Poke is a low-pressure, calming, and relaxing felt-style reveal game built for short, satisfying, stress-relief-friendly sessions.`

## 7. What To Track

For each target, track:

- date sent
- site
- contact email or form
- status
- reply
- coverage URL
- whether they linked to the site or App Store
- whether they used phrases like `calming`, `cozy`, `relaxing`, `reveal`, or `felt-style`

Suggested statuses:

- `not started`
- `sent`
- `followed up`
- `replied`
- `covered`
- `declined`
