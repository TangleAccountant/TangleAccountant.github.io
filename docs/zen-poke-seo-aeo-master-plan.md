# Zen Poke SEO + AEO Master Plan

Last updated: March 31, 2026

This document is the working SEO, AEO, and organic growth plan for `funnymanfinance.com` and Zen Poke.

It combines:

- The live-site audit
- The local repo audit
- Business goals and audience answers from Andrew
- Official Google and OpenAI guidance
- The additional X-thread material supplied in this project

The purpose of this document is to act as the source of truth before rewriting pages, adding new landing pages, setting up Search Console, and setting up GA4.

## 1. Business Goal

Primary goal:

- Reach `10,000 downloads on iOS`
- Reach `10,000 downloads on Android`

Secondary goals:

- Make the website primarily a `Zen Poke + personal portfolio` site
- Increase organic discovery from both `traditional search` and `AI answer engines`
- Build a foundation that can support both the current App Store listing and the Google Play launch

## 2. What We Know About The Product And Audience

Confirmed inputs:

- Zen Poke appeals to both men and women, but `women across age groups respond more strongly`
- The strongest existing audience appears to skew toward `middle-aged to older women`
- The experience is `casual`, `satisfying`, `low-cognitive-load`, and `puzzle-adjacent`
- The product should be positioned around `calming`, `stress relief`, and feeling mentally lighter
- The game is `not actually handmade`, so the site should not describe it as handmade
- The closest competition is not one exact game, but the broader space of `casual`, `cozy`, and `satisfying puzzle games`

Implication:

- Public copy should use language like `felt art`, `felt-style`, `cozy`, `soft`, `calming`, `satisfying`, `phone-friendly`, `offline-friendly`, `daily puzzle`, and `short sessions`
- Public copy should avoid claiming the product is `handmade`
- Public copy should avoid medical or therapeutic claims like `treats anxiety`, `improves depression`, or `mental health treatment`

Safe framing:

- `calming`
- `relaxing`
- `good for unwinding`
- `helps you slow down`
- `a healthier break from endless scrolling`
- `light, low-pressure play`

Unsafe framing:

- `treats anxiety`
- `improves mental health disorders`
- `medical-grade stress reduction`
- `clinically proven to improve mental health`

## 3. Core Positioning Recommendation

Zen Poke should be positioned as:

`A relaxing and calming mobile game for iPhone, iPad, and Android that gives players a soft, low-pressure, tactile-feeling way to unwind through satisfying felt-style reveals in short sessions.`

This is stronger than trying to win generic terms like:

- `mobile game`
- `puzzle game`
- `fun game`
- `best game`

The SEO target is not just broad traffic. The target is:

- the right people
- with the right intent
- landing on the right page
- and clicking through to download

## 4. High-Level SEO Thesis

The site should stop acting like a lightly branded landing page collection and start acting like a clearly structured app entity site.

The site needs to become:

- easy for Google to crawl and refresh
- easy for AI systems to understand and cite
- easy for humans to understand within seconds
- easy to navigate from broad intent to app-download intent

In practical terms:

- The homepage should orient around `Zen Poke first`, `portfolio second`
- Each page should have its own keyword cluster and search intent
- The site should contain a small but deliberate Zen Poke content hub
- Important facts about the game and publisher should be stated in clean, machine-readable formats
- The site should include enough direct-answer content that AI systems can lift and cite it

Indexation signal gaps:

- Search engines have weak crawl/update hints right now

- The lack of canonical and structured signals makes it easier for the site to look thin or less authoritative than it really is

## 6. What The Live Search Environment Suggests

Observed conditions:

- Zen Poke is not yet dominating its own branded search space
- Broad generic terms are crowded and not the right first battle
- The opportunity is strongest in mid-tail and long-tail discovery queries
- The opportunity is also strong in answer-engine prompts where users describe the outcome they want rather than search the app name

Implication:

The strategy should not be:

- write vague SEO blog posts
- chase massive head terms first
- build 10,000 thin pages immediately

The strategy should be:

- own the brand/entity
- own the descriptive product category around the game
- own the use-case phrases around calm play, short sessions, satisfying play, phone-friendly play, and offline-friendly puzzle sessions
- publish direct-answer pages and structured comparison/explainer content

## 7. Keyword Strategy Principles

Keywords matter at the `page level`, not only on the homepage.

Each page should target:

- one primary keyword cluster
- two to four secondary/supporting phrases

The homepage should target:

- brand + entity + publisher intent

The app page should target:

- the strongest commercial-intent app discovery terms

Supporting pages should target:

- feature-based queries
- question-based queries
- platform-based queries

### Recommended app/entity file

Create:

- `/.well-known/brand-facts.json`

Adapted for Zen Poke, it should include:

- app name
- publisher name
- platform availability
- App Store URL
- Google Play URL once live
- support contact
- privacy URL
- terms URL
- category labels
- short factual description
- last updated date

This is not an official guaranteed ranking factor. It is still worth doing because it makes the entity and app facts easier for machines to consume.

### Rendering and HTML order implications

One X thread correctly highlighted that Googlebot rendering is constrained and HTML order matters.

For this site, the practical implication is:

- keep titles, canonicals, meta descriptions, OG tags, and JSON-LD high in the document head
- keep critical page meaning in plain HTML, not injected later
- keep major headings and direct-answer content near the top of the body

Because the site is already static HTML, Zen Poke is in an excellent position to benefit from this.

## 14. AEO Strategy For Zen Poke

The useful durable lesson from the long AEO X thread is not the exact revenue claims.

The durable lesson is:

- AI systems prefer direct, factual, well-structured, easy-to-cite pages
- AI systems are more likely to mention brands they can verify cleanly
- AI systems prefer answer-oriented pages over vague marketing pages

### 14.1 Build An Answer Intent Map

Create a recurring spreadsheet of questions people might ask:

- what is Zen Poke
- what is a relaxing puzzle game for iPhone
- what are calm games for adults
- what are satisfying mobile games for short breaks
- what are good offline puzzle games
- what games help you unwind without too much thinking
- what are cozy mobile games for women
- what are daily puzzle apps that feel relaxing

For each prompt, track:

- whether Zen Poke appears
- how it is described
- what competitors are mentioned
- what sources get cited

This becomes the feedback loop for future content.

### 14.2 Build Answer-Ready Pages

Each important page should contain:

- a short TL;DR paragraph near the top
- direct language that cleanly answers the page topic
- a few factual bullets
- a lightweight FAQ section where appropriate

This helps both humans and AI systems.

### 14.3 Build A Brand Facts Page

Recommended page:

- `/zen-poke/brand-facts/`

Contents:

- short neutral description of the game

- app category
- supported platforms
- pricing model

Tone:

- factual

This page is not for persuasion. It is for verification.

### Thread: AI assistants can become a major commerce/discovery channel

Useful takeaway:

- Yes, AI recommendation traffic can become meaningful

- publish clean, direct, structured pages

### Thread: Medium canonical republishing and Blogger backlinks

Useful takeaway:

- Repurposing strong content onto larger platforms can help reach and citation discovery
- Canonical tagging matters when republishing

What to avoid:

- treating Medium or Blogger as a main SEO strategy
- chasing low-value backlinks for their own sake
- spammy syndication

Recommended use:

- selectively republish one or two of the strongest Zen Poke explainers on Medium with proper canonical tags
- use this as distribution support, not as the core plan

### Thread: Distribution matters more than pure building

Useful takeaway:

- correct
- the site should not only explain the app, it should distribute the app

Best ideas from that thread for Zen Poke:

- answer-engine optimization
- AI-assisted content repurposing
- free tools only if they truly fit the audience
- shareable artifacts if the game can produce them

Bad fit right now:

- `10,000 SEO pages built once`
- buying a newsletter before the funnel is proven
- building an MCP server as a top priority before the website and app entity are fixed

### Thread: Googlebot fetch/render constraints and HTML order

Useful takeaway:

- important content and markup should not be buried
- stateless rendering means cookie/session dependent content can be problematic
- static HTML is an advantage

Zen Poke application:

- keep important facts in raw HTML
- avoid hiding page meaning behind JS
- keep metadata and JSON-LD clean and early

## 16. Programmatic SEO: Use Carefully

The large distribution thread promotes programmatic SEO and 10,000-page builds.

For Zen Poke:

- Do not start with 10,000 pages
- Do not publish hundreds of thin pages
- Do not create city pages or generic keyword permutations with no unique value

Recommended stance:

- Start with 10 to 20 high-quality Zen Poke pages
- Validate indexation and traffic
- Expand only where there is repeated intent and real value

If programmatic SEO is used later, it should be for:

- release notes
- well-structured FAQ expansions
- perhaps category pages around puzzle themes if there is real content
